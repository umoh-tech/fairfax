Common Questions

Answers to What You're Probably Thinking

How quickly can you respond to an emergency in Los Angeles? +

For most of our service area, we can have an electrician on-site within 1–2 hours of your call. We operate a true 24/7 emergency line — not a message service. Someone will pick up, assess your situation, and dispatch immediately if needed.

Are you licensed and insured in California? +

Yes. All our electricians hold current California electrical contractor licenses and we carry full general liability and workers' compensation insurance. We're happy to provide proof of insurance before starting any job — just ask.

Do you pull permits for panel upgrades and rewiring? +

Absolutely. Any work that requires a permit — panel upgrades, rewiring, new circuits — we handle the permit process through the City of LA. Skipping permits is how homeowners end up with problems when selling their home or filing insurance claims. We do it right.

How much does a panel upgrade cost in Los Angeles? +

Panel upgrades in LA typically range from $1,800 to $3,500 depending on your current panel size, the amperage you're upgrading to, and whether the utility needs to disconnect service. We provide detailed, itemized quotes before starting — no vague estimates.

My house was built in the 1950s. Do I need to rewire it? +

Not always, but it's worth an inspection. Homes built before 1960 often have knob-and-tube wiring that can't handle modern electrical loads and may not be insurable. We'll assess your wiring honestly and tell you what actually needs to be done — not upsell you on work you don't need.

Do you install EV chargers for Tesla and other electric vehicles? +

Yes. We install Level 2 EV chargers (240V / 40–50 amp circuits) compatible with Tesla, Rivian, GM, Ford, Hyundai, and all other EV brands. We handle the permit, install the dedicated circuit, mount the charger, and schedule the inspection.

What if I'm a landlord with multiple rental units? +

We work with property managers and landlords throughout LA County on a regular basis. We can coordinate directly with tenants, document all work for your records, and offer priority scheduling for property portfolios. Call to discuss a service agreement.

Panel Upgrade Los Angeles

Is Your Panel Holding You Back?

Most homes built before 1990 were wired for 60 or 100 amps — enough for the appliances of that era. Today's homes run EV chargers, heat pumps, electric ranges, and dozens of always-on devices. If your breakers trip constantly, you can't run two appliances simultaneously, or your panel still has a fuse box, you're overdue for an upgrade.

An undersized panel isn't just inconvenient — it's a fire hazard. Overloaded circuits overheat. And many insurance providers in California are beginning to decline coverage on homes with outdated electrical panels.

What the Upgrade Involves

We upgrade your service from 100-amp to 200-amp (or higher) by replacing the main panel, meter base if needed, and all associated wiring. The process includes:

  • Initial assessment and load calculation
  • Permit application through the City of Los Angeles
  • Coordination with LADWP for service disconnect
  • Installation of new main breaker panel with proper grounding
  • Transfer of all existing circuits to new panel
  • City inspection — we manage this entirely

What You Get

  • Reliable power for all modern appliances and EVs
  • Lower risk of electrical fires from overloaded circuits
  • Insurance compliance for most California providers
  • Increased home value — buyers know what a new panel means
  • Capacity to add solar, battery backup, or EV charging

Pricing Guidance

Panel upgrades in Los Angeles range from $1,800 – $3,500 for a standard 200-amp upgrade, depending on the age of your existing panel, whether a sub-panel is involved, and current LADWP requirements. We provide a fully itemized quote — no surprises on the final bill.

We don't lowball quotes to win jobs and charge more at completion. What you approve is what you pay.

Panel Upgrade FAQs

How long does it take?
Most panel upgrades take 4–8 hours. Your power will be off for part of that time. We schedule around your availability.

Do I need to be home?
Yes — someone 18+ needs to be present for permit inspection and to restore access. We'll coordinate timing to minimize inconvenience.

Will it increase my homeowner's insurance?
In most cases, a panel upgrade will reduce your insurance premium or resolve a coverage denial. Ask your insurer for a "completed upgrades" discount.
